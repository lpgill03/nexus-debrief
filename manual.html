Platform Overview

Nexus Investing operates on the principle that the best trading decisions emerge from diverse, specialized intelligence working in concert. The platform's eight-agent council includes:

  • Market Analyst — Processes technical indicators, price action, and chart patterns
  • Fundamentals Analyst — Evaluates company financial health and valuation metrics
  • News & Sentiment Analyst — Grades market catalysts and investor sentiment
  • Calendar Agent — Tracks earnings, economic events, and market-moving dates
  • Bull Researcher — Constructs the optimistic investment thesis
  • Bear Researcher — Constructs the pessimistic counterargument
  • Risk Manager — Identifies portfolio-level threats and position risks
  • Trader Agent (CIO) — Synthesizes all inputs and renders final decisions

Each agent uses the optimal AI model for its specific task, following the "70-20-10 Rule" for cost efficiency: 70% of processing happens on fast, economical models for routine analysis; 20% on mid-tier models for nuanced tasks; and just 10% on premium models like Claude Opus 4.5 for critical final decisions.

V98 Strategic Evolution

The decision to pivot toward premium selling was driven by cold, hard data. A comprehensive backtest spanning 504 trading days revealed stark performance differences between strategies. Traditional call buying generated a 13% win rate—meaning 87% of trades lost money. The occasional winners couldn't offset accumulated losses.

By contrast, the V98 premium selling strategies achieved:

  • 90.1% win rate on cash-governed short strangles (81 trades)
  • 100% win rate on directional credit spreads (80 trades)
  • $15,901 net profit over the same period that previous strategies lost $5,458

The fundamental advantage lies in time decay (theta). When you buy options, time works against you—every passing day erodes value. When you sell options, time works for you—every passing day brings you closer to keeping the premium collected. By selling far out-of-the-money options with low probability of assignment, the platform achieves high win rates while limiting downside risk.

Risk Management Philosophy

Nexus employs multiple layers of protection:

  • Position Limits: Maximum allocation per trade prevents overconcentration
  • Circuit Breakers: Automatic trading halt when daily loss limits are reached
  • Stop Losses: Every position has predefined exit points based on technical levels or ATR
  • VIX Filters: Regime detection adjusts strategy when volatility exceeds thresholds
  • Correlation Limits: Portfolio-level monitoring prevents excessive correlation exposure
  • Loss Streak Protection: Position sizes automatically reduce after consecutive losses

The Risk Manager agent reviews every proposed trade before execution and can veto high-risk opportunities regardless of council enthusiasm.
